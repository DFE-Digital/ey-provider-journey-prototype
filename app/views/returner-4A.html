What does non-permanent mean?

In the six months before starting their current role, your employee must not have had a permanent role working directly with children in an early years setting.

If they did work in early years in the 6 months before starting their current role, you can claim if this was in the following types of employment:

  • voluntary or unpaid work
  • casual and temporary

Casual or temporary work could include:

  • zero-hour contracts
  • guaranteed minimum hour contracts
  • self-employed or freelance contracts
  • fixed term contracts (FTCs)
  • agency work and apprenticeship roles
