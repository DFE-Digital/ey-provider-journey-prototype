What is the early years financial incentive?

The early years financial incentive is a new scheme to support nurseries with hiring new practitioners.

It will enable some nurseries to advertise jobs with a £1000 sign-on bonus attached.

The scheme will apply to nurseries that offer free childcare entitlements, in particular local authority areas.

Who will receive the payment?

Most people who are new to working in early years or are returning to the profession can receive the £1000 payment. Some extra eligibility criteria will apply depending on their role and previous experience.

What happens before the recruitment stage?

The local authority will have a set number of financial incentives that these nurseries can use to recruit new staff.

They will also be directed to guidance explaining that they will need to use a digital service to submit claims for payment on behalf of new practitioners. They will be directed to a link to this service.

Nurseries will provide an email address to their local authority to be used to log in to the digital service.

How will the payment process work?

When a practitioner has been recruited for a role with an incentive attached, the nursery will use the digital service to submit a claim for the £1000 payment on the practitioner’s behalf.

When the nursery has submitted the claim, the practitioner will receive an email asking them to add their payment details.

The practitioner will then receive the one-off £1000 payment after they have been in the role for 6 months. This will be paid directly to the practitioner by the Department for Education.
