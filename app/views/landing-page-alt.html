Claim an early years financial incentive payment

The purpose of this incentive scheme is to increase the number of people working with children in permanent roles, within the early years sector.

Use this service to make an early years financial incentive payment claim for an employee.

Eligibility

To make a claim for an employee, they must:

  • have applied for a job with an incentive attached
  • have started their job after 15 May 2024
  • not have had a permanent job working directly with children in an early years setting in the 6 months before starting their current job
  • spend most of the time in their job (70% or more) working directly with children

Working directly with children can involve any of the following:

  • working in the room with children — this can include playrooms, baserooms and classrooms
  • guiding, supporting and interacting with children in their learning and development
  • giving hands-on care, including health and hygiene

70% or more time applies to all early years foundation stage (EYFS) part and full-time roles, and there are no minimum number of contracted hours.

Employees who have worked in the 6 months before starting their current job

If your employee has worked in an early years setting in the 6 months before starting their current job, you can claim if their work was:

  • voluntary or unpaid
  • casual and temporary

Casual or temporary work could include:

  • zero-hour contracts
  • guaranteed minimum hour contracts
  • self-employed or freelance contracts
  • fixed term contracts (FTCs)
  • agency work and apprenticeship jobs

You cannot make a claim if they had a permanent job working directly with children in an early years setting.

You can find more information about employment contracts on the ACAS website.

More information can be found in the guidance.

Information you'll need to provide

You’ll need to provide some details about your employee and the company or organisation the employee works for. You'll be doing this on behalf of the company or organisation.

Information from your employee

You should make sure you’ve collected written consent from your employee before you give us their information. You can use our consent form to do this.

You'll need their:

  • full name
  • email address
  • start date

You must also provide your employee with a privacy notice that explains what information will be collected, why it is being collected and who it will be shared with. You can use our privacy notice for this.

We’ll also ask you to confirm that they meet the eligibility criteria.

Cancel or get help with your claim

If your employee leaves their job before they’ve been in it for 6 months, you should cancel their claim.

You can cancel a claim or get help on this service by contacting us at help@opsteam.education.gov.uk
