Overview

This scheme enables nurseries to receive a number of £1000 payments to attach to job adverts to recruit permanent practitioners. These can be used to recruit for part and full-time roles.

The aim is to support nurseries with recruitment and increase the number of new permanent practitioners in the early years workforce.

The scheme runs from 25 November 2024 to 15 March 2025.

Local authorities participating in this scheme

You can only participate in this scheme if your nursery is in one of these local authorities:

  • Barking and Dagenham
  • Coventry
  • Gateshead
  • Redcar and Cleveland
  • Telford and Wrekin
  • Thurrock

These local authorities were chosen because they have:

  • high levels of deprivation
  • low sufficiency levels (the number of available nursery places compared to the number of children who need a nursery place)

How it works

From 25 November 2024 to 15 March 2025, nurseries in participating local authorities can use financial incentives to recruit staff. They can either be:

  • new to the sector
  • returning after a break of at least 6 months
  • moving from a temporary to permanent contract

After you’ve recruited an eligible new employee, you need to start a claim on their behalf on a digital service. The employee will then receive an email inviting them to complete the claim.

After 6 months you’ll be asked to confirm that they are still in their role. If they are, they will then receive the £1000 payment.

How money is allocated

The Department for Education has allocated funding to enable you to request a number of £1000 payments to use to recruit staff.

Payments will be allocated to nurseries in the order they are requested (on a first come, first served basis). We’ll email participating nurseries when the number of allocations are running low.

You will receive a link via email when the service opens which you can use to request allocations.

Nursery eligibility

Your nursery is eligible if:

  • it's in a participating local authority
  • you have been contacted by the Department for Education and told you're eligible to offer payments
  • you’re currently implementing the early years foundation stage (but are not running a reception class or childminding setting)

Practitioner eligibility

Applicants who are new or returning to early years are eligible if:

  • they have not worked in a permanent role in a nursery in the 6 months before starting their new role, where they spent most of their time (70% or more) working directly with children
  • the role they’ve applied for is a permanent contract (this applies to part and full-time roles)
  • they’ll spend most of the time (70% or more) in their role working directly with children

Applicants do not need to have any early years qualifications or have worked in a nursery before.

You cannot offer the incentive to anyone who:

  • has worked in a permanent role in a nursery in the last 6 months
  • will not be spending most of their time (70% or more) working directly with children in their new role

Working directly with children

Working directly with children can involve any of the following:

  • working in the room with children — this can include playrooms, base rooms and classrooms
  • guiding, supporting and interacting with children in their learning and development
  • giving hands-on care, including health and hygiene

70% or more time applies to all part and full-time roles. The employee does not need to work a minimum number of contracted hours to be eligible.

Apprenticeships

Apprentices cannot receive a payment, even if they’re on a permanent contract during their apprenticeship.

Apprentices can receive a payment when they move from being an apprentice to an employee. To do this, they must have passed their apprenticeship and been taken on in a permanent role.

How practitioners are paid

The Department for Education will pay the money directly into the employee’s bank account. This will happen after they have been in the role for 6 months.

Tax and deductions

The Department for Education will pay the employee’s Income Tax and National Insurance contributions.

There may be other deductions based on the employee’s circumstances. For example, if they’re making student loan repayments. This could reduce the final amount of the incentive below £1000.

Universal Credit

If the employee claims Universal Credit, the £1000 payment may affect the amount of Universal Credit they receive.

Read more about how changes to earnings can affect Universal Credit.

Student loan

The £1000 payment will not be affected by any funding they have previously received to study for a college course or apprenticeship.

If they have previously studied at university and have a student loan, their £1000 payment may affect their repayments.

If they’re currently repaying a student loan

If they’re currently repaying a student loan, a deduction towards it may come out of their £1000 payment.

If they have a student loan but are not currently repaying it

If their payment takes them over the amount they must earn to start paying back a student loan, a deduction may go towards repaying it.

Read more about when they'll start paying back a student loan and what they'll pay.

They may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how employees can claim back student loan deductions.

If the employee leaves before they’ve been in the role for 6 months

You need to tell us by email if your employee leaves your nursery before they have completed 6 months. You can do this by contacting us at opsteam@education.gov.uk.

The employee must complete 6 months in their role to receive the payment. We'll contact you by email before that point to check your employee is still working in their role.
