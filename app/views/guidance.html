Claim an early years incentive payment: new and returning educators

About the scheme

The Department for Education (DfE) is offering a £1,000 financial incentive to new and returning educators. This is to increase the number of staff in nurseries and early years providers.

Eligible nurseries can apply for the £1,000 bonus on behalf of new and returning educators from September 2025.

DfE reserves the bonuses for eligible nurseries in the order in which they receive them.

We will notify nurseries when bonuses are running low and when the application window will close.

To be eligible for a payment as an early years educator, you must:

  • have applied for a role with an attached incentive at an eligible nursery
  • be on a permanent contract
  • have started in your role on 15 July 2025 or later
  • spend most of the time (70% or more) in your role working directly with children

Working directly with children can involve any of the following:

  • working in the room with children, including playrooms, base rooms and classrooms
  • guiding, supporting and interacting with children in their learning and development
  • giving hands-on care including health and hygiene

You’re also eligible for the scheme as an early years educator if you:

  • are new to the sector
  • are moving from a temporary contract or apprenticeship to a permanent contract
  • are returning after a break of at least 6 months
  • are on either a permanent part-time or full-time contract – there is not a minimum amount of hours you need to work
  • have not worked in a permanent nursery role in the 6 months prior to starting your new role, where you have spent most of your time (70% or more) working directly with children
  • have no early years qualifications or have not worked in a nursery before

You’re not eligible if you:

  • are an apprentice, even if you’re on a permanent contract during your apprenticeship – once you complete your apprenticeship and move into a permanent non-apprenticeship role, you will become eligible
  • started your employment before 15 July 2025 and have returned from a long period of leave, such as maternity leave
  • are on a non-permanent contract such as temporary, voluntary or agency contract

How to apply

Early years providers start the claim on behalf of an early years educator. DfE will then email the educator to complete the claim.

  • applied for a role with an incentive attached
  • started in their new role between [eligibility window start date] and [eligibility window end date]

EY educators can either be:

  • new to the sector
  • moving from a temporary contract or apprenticeship to a permanent contract
  • returning after a break of at least 6 months

Once an EY educator has started their new role:

  1. The nursery needs to start a claim on their behalf.
  2. The money will be reserved for the EY educator and they will receive an email inviting them to complete the claim.
  3. After 6 months, the nursery will be asked via email to confirm that the EY educator is still in their role.
  4. If the EY educator is still in their role, DfE will pay the recruitment bonus directly into their bank account.

If an EY educator receives a recruitment bonus and then leaves their job, they can apply for the payment again in the future. They must meet the following conditions to be eligible:

  • in the 6 months before applying again, they cannot work directly with children for 70% or more of the time in their new role
  • after becoming eligible, they must wait another 6 months before receiving the payment

Eligible early years educators

EY educators who are new or returning to an early years setting are eligible if:

  • the role they’ve applied for is a permanent part-time or full-time contract - they do not need to work a minimum number of contracted hours to be eligible
  • they’ll spend most of the time (70% or more) in their role working directly with children
  • they have not worked in a permanent nursery role in the 6 months prior to starting their new role, where they spent most of their time (70% or more) working directly with children

Applicants do not need to have any early years qualifications or have worked in a nursery before.

EY educators are not eligible for a £1,000 recruitment bonus if they are:

  • an employee who has returned from a long period of leave, such as maternity leave
  • on a non-permanent contract - such as a temporary, voluntary or agency contract

Apprenticeships

Recruitment bonuses are not available to apprentices, even if they have a permanent contract during their apprenticeship.

Apprentices can receive a recruitment bonus when they move from being an apprentice to an employee. To qualify, they must:

  • complete their apprenticeship
  • be taken on in a permanent part-time or full-time role

Working directly with children

Working directly with children can involve any of the following:

  • working in the room with children - this can include playrooms, base rooms and classrooms
  • guiding, supporting and interacting with children in their learning and development
  • giving hands-on care, including health and hygiene

Income Tax, National Insurance and pensions

We’ll pay National Insurance and basic rate Income Tax for the recruitment bonus on behalf of the EY educator. If an EY educator earns £50,270 or more per year, they’ll need to pay any Income Tax at the higher rate through PAYE.

The bonus is not part of the EY educator’s salary from their employer. The EY educator, the nursery or the government will not make a contribution to the EY educator’s pension as part of the payment.

The recruitment bonus, along with the Income Tax and National Insurance contributions made on the EY educator’s behalf, will count towards their income. EY educators should consider how this will affect any other benefits or tax credits they receive, such as Universal Credit.

Student loan

The £1,000 recruitment bonus will not be affected by any funding the EY educator has previously received to study for a college course or apprenticeship.

If the EY educator has previously studied at university and has a student loan, the following conditions apply:

  • if they’re currently repaying a student loan, a deduction towards it may come out of their £1,000 recruitment bonus
  • if their recruitment bonus takes them over the amount they must earn to start paying back a student loan, a deduction may go towards repaying it

Read more about when an EY educator will start paying back a student loan (opens in new tab) and what they’ll pay (opens in new tab).

They may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how EY educators can claim back student loan deductions (opens in new tab).
