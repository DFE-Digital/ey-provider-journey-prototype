Overview

This scheme enables you to receive a number of £1000 payments to attach to job adverts to recruit practitioners. These can be used to recruit for part and full-time roles.

The aim is to support nurseries with recruitment and increase the number of permanent practitioners in the early years sector.

How it works

Your local authority will have a set number of financial incentives that you and other nurseries in your local authority can use to recruit staff.

After you’ve recruited a new employee, you need to start a claim on their behalf on a digital service. The employee will then receive an email inviting them to complete the claim.

The employee will receive the £1000 payment after they’ve been in their role for 6 months.

How money is allocated

Your local authority will have a set number of £1000 payments to allocate to nurseries in the area. You will need to apply for payments from your local authority based on your need.

Payments will be allocated to nurseries in the order they are requested (on a first come, first served basis).

Nursery eligibility

Your nursery is eligible if:

  • it's in [named local authority areas]
  • you have been contacted and told the nursery is eligible to receive payments
  • it offers free childcare entitlements

Practitioner eligibility

Applicants who are new or returning to early years are eligible if:

  • they have not worked in a permanent role in a nursery in the 6 months before starting their new role, where they spent most of their time (70% or more) working directly with children
  • the role they’ve applied for is a permanent contract (this applies to part and full-time roles)
  • they’ll spend most of the time (70% or more) in their role working directly with children

Applicants do not need to have had any early years training or worked in a nursery before.

You cannot offer the incentive to anyone who:

  • has worked in a permanent role in a nursery in the last 6 months
  • will not be spending most of their time (70% or more) working directly with children in their new role
