Additional payments for teaching: eligibility and payment details

We give additional payments to some teachers to teach certain subjects in areas that have a high need for teachers.

Documents

Early career payments for teachers

Guidance

The Department for Education will pay the employee’s Income Tax and National Insurance contributions.

There may be other deductions based on the employee’s circumstances. For example, if they’re making student loan repayments. This could reduce the final amount of the incentive below £1000.

Universal Credit

If the employee claims Universal Credit, the £1000 payment may affect the amount of Universal Credit they receive.

Read more about how changes to earnings can affect Universal Credit.

Student loan

The £1000 payment will not be affected by any funding they have previously received to study for a college course or apprenticeship.

If they have previously studied at university and have a student loan, their £1000 payment may affect their repayments.

If they’re currently repaying a student loan

If they’re currently repaying a student loan, a deduction towards it may come out of their £1000 payment.

If they have a student loan but are not currently repaying it

If their payment takes them over the amount they must earn to start paying back a student loan, a deduction may go towards repaying it.

Read more about when they'll start paying back a student loan and what they'll pay.

They may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how employees can claim back student loan deductions.

If the employee leaves before they’ve been in the role for 6 months

You need to tell us by email if your employee leaves your nursery before they have completed 6 months. You can do this by contacting us at opsteam@education.gov.uk.

The employee must complete 6 months in their role to receive the payment. We'll contact you by email before that point to check your employee is still working in their role.
